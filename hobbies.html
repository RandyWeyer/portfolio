Game Development

Currently I'm working on a 2D RPG that uses the RPG Maker MV engine. I'm planning on making it look and feel somewhat like Final Fantasy VI with a quest system similar to the Elder Scrolls: Oblivion. There might also be some Zelda: Breath of the Wild aspects to the gameplay as well.

The most important thing that I've learned while developing a game is that it takes a lot of people with a diverse set of talents to make any game. Not only is there programming, but visual artists, music. You might not think of it while you're playing a game, but someone has to perfectly polish those half-second sounds that play while you're navigating the menu.

Although it's been a passion project, it's also been a huge learning lesson in what can be accomplished in a given time period and constantly doing triage on every single aspect of the game. I could spend a whole day writing descriptions in plain text for items, or I could spend the same amount of time finding royalty-free music. As the sole developer I have to constantly juggle all of these duties.
